Artists

Masters of the Golden Seam

Traditional Masters

Japanese Masters

Nobuyasu Suginaka

Traditional Kintsugi Master, Kyoto

A third-generation lacquerware artisan in Kyoto, Suginaka has spent over forty years perfecting the traditional Hon-Kintsugi method.

Seisaku Nakajima

Lacquer Artist & Restorer

Nakajima specialises in the restoration of historic tea ceramics, applying Kintsugi only where the original craftsmen would have sanctioned it.

漆

Keiko Hata

Ceramic Conservator, Tokyo National Museum

One of the few women to reach the highest levels of traditional Kintsugi, Hata has restored pieces from the Edo period.

金

Maki Mizuno

Contemporary Kintsugi Artist, Osaka

Mizuno blends traditional technique with a contemporary visual language, applying Kintsugi to mass-produced ceramics to raise questions about value and perfection.

Rio Hashimoto

Kintsugi Educator & Author

Hashimoto has written two influential books on Kintsugi technique and runs workshops across Japan and internationally.

Contemporary Artists

Beyond the Studio

Yoko Ono

Conceptual Artist

Yoko Ono's ceramic collection, particularly her ongoing project Mend Piece (first shown in 1966), invites participants to mend broken cups using any available material.

Tomomi Kamoshita

Contemporary Ceramicist

Known for her series Gift From The Waves, Kamoshita creates ceramics that appear to have already been broken and repaired.
Kintsugi in Turkey

Artists from Turkey

Özlem Kevser Birkan

Kintsugi Master & Educator, Istanbul

Özlem Kevser Birkan is widely credited with bringing authentic Kintsugi practice to Turkey. Her path began with a deep interest in Japanese aesthetics, leading her to train in Japan under traditional masters. On returning to Istanbul, she founded her studio and teaching practice at Lotus5, introducing both the technique and the Wabi-Sabi philosophy to a new audience.
